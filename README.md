# Enhancing Gym Profitability by Optimizing Member Retention & Acquisition

**BDM Capstone Project — IITM BS Degree Program, IIT Madras**

**Author:** Daksh (Roll No. 23F2001398)

## Overview

This project is a data-driven consulting study conducted for **F4 Fitness Gym**, a local B2C gym located in Pochanpur Village, Sector-23, Dwarka, Delhi, founded by Mr. Ajay Tomar in 2016. The gym was facing two core business problems — **poor member retention/acquisition** and **overcrowding during peak hours** — both of which were affecting profitability and the overall member experience.

Using eight months of real operational data (January–August 2024) collected directly from the gym's records, the project analyzes membership trends, member behavior, gender distribution, and time-slot-wise crowd patterns to uncover the root causes of these problems and translate the findings into concrete, actionable business recommendations.

## Organization Background

- **Gym:** F4 Fitness
- **Owner:** Mr. Ajay Tomar
- **Location:** Pochanpur Village, Sector-23, Dwarka, Delhi
- **Established:** 2016
- **Operating hours:** 12 hours/day in two shifts (4 AM–10 AM & 4 PM–10 PM), 6 days a week
- **Annual turnover:** ~₹10 lakh (pre-analysis)

## Problem Statement

1. **Member Retention & Acquisition** — The gym struggles to retain existing members and attract new ones.
2. **Overcrowding** — The gym experiences overcrowding during specific hours of the day, degrading the member experience and contributing to churn.

### Root Causes Identified
- Insufficient equipment relative to member count during peak hours, leading to long wait times.
- Limited physical space with no scope for expansion (flats adjacent to the gym).
- No dedicated parking, limiting the gym's reach to a very local catchment.
- Competition from newer gyms, online fitness alternatives, and nearby established chains.
- Underpriced/under-differentiated trainer services compared to competitors.

## Data

Data was collected directly from F4 Fitness Gym's internal records with owner consent (proof of originality included: signed letter, photos, and a recorded interview with the owner).

| Dataset | Period Covered | Key Fields |
|---|---|---|
| **Demographics Data** | 1 month (August 2024) | Member ID, Membership Type, Age, Gender, Address, Distance from Gym, Monthly Fees |
| **Member Behavior Data** | 1 month (August 2024) | Member ID, Check-In Time, Check-Out Time, Duration |
| **Member Distribution Data** | 8 months (Jan–Aug 2024) | Gender distribution by month, Membership subscription type distribution |
| **Overcrowding Data** | 1 month (August 2024) | Time Slot, Area/Zone, Equipment in Use, Number of Members, Member Comments/Ratings |

Data was cleaned in Microsoft Excel (removing duplicate entries and irrelevant columns) and analyzed using Google Sheets/Excel functions and standard charting (line, pie, and column charts).

## Methodology

1. **Data Collection** — Structured interviews and record extraction from the gym's admission/attendance registers.
2. **Data Cleaning** — Removed duplicates and irrelevant fields to ensure analysis-ready datasets.
3. **Data Analysis** —
   - Line charts to track membership subscription trends and gender distribution over 8 months.
   - Pie charts for gender ratio and peak vs. off-peak visit distribution.
   - Column charts for check-in time distribution to identify peak/off-peak hours.
   - Tabular analysis of equipment usage and member satisfaction ratings across time slots.
4. **Interpretation** — Findings were interpreted in the context of the gym's local, community-driven customer base and seasonal patterns (school/college exam cycles).
5. **Recommendations** — Translated findings into practical, low-cost interventions the owner could realistically implement.

## Key Findings

**Membership Trends**
- Total revenue collected over 8 months: **₹11,61,600**.
- **Monthly plans** are the most popular but also have the **highest dropout rate**.
- **Quarterly and annual** subscribers show significantly higher retention.

**Overcrowding**
- Peak hours: **6 AM–9 AM** and **6 PM–9 PM** (most crowded: 7–8 PM).
- Off-peak hours: 4–6 AM, 9–10 AM, 4–6 PM, 9–10 PM.
- **81.67%** of visits occur during peak hours vs. just **18.33%** during off-peak hours.
- Equipment waiting time during peak hours is a leading driver of member dissatisfaction and dropout.

**Gender Distribution**
- Members are consistently **63% male / 37% female** across all 8 months — indicating untapped potential in the female membership segment.

**Seasonality**
- Membership **rises in March, June, and July** (school/college exam completions and vacations).
- Membership **dips in April–May** (reopening of academic institutions).

## Recommendations

**Retention & Acquisition**
- Loyalty programs for monthly members (fee discounts, trainer-fee discounts, bonus days for long-term continuity).
- Seasonal promotions targeting students during peak months (March, June, July).
- Local business collaborations offering discounted corporate memberships.
- Flexible plans, e.g. pay-per-visit (~₹100/day) for casual/short-term users.
- Free health & wellness workshops and one-week free trials with a demo trainer session for new members.
- Structured member feedback collection to proactively address concerns.

**Managing Overcrowding**
- Discounted fees for members who use off-peak slots.
- Group classes (yoga, Zumba) scheduled during off-peak hours.
- Additional trainers/staff during peak hours for better service and crowd management.
- Gym layout redesign to relocate high-demand equipment to underutilized zones.
- Extended operating hours to spread out member traffic.

## Expected Outcomes

- Increased member retention and acquisition through loyalty and referral-driven growth.
- Projected annual turnover growth from ~₹10 lakh to **₹12–14 lakh**.
- Reduced overcrowding via better scheduling and off-peak incentives.
- Improved member satisfaction through structured feedback loops and service enhancements.
